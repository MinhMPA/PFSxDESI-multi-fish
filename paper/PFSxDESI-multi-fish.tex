\author[a,b,c]{Nhat-Minh Nguyen (Nguyễn Nhật Minh)\orcidlink{0000-0002-2542-7233}}

\abstract{%
Full-shape analyses of galaxy clustering in the effective field theory
of large-scale structure (EFTofLSS) have emerged as a powerful probe
of cosmological parameters---but marginalizing over \(\sim\!12\) nuisance
parameters per tracer per redshift bin remains the dominant source of
degradation.
%
Recent work has demonstrated that informative priors on these parameters
can substantially sharpen constraints: HOD-informed priors improve
\(\sigma(\se)\) by \(\sim\!23\%\) [Zhang et al.\ 2025], and field-level
simulation-based priors by \(\sim\!50\%\) [Chudaykin, Ivanov \& Philcox 2026].
%
However, all existing approaches rely on halo-occupation-distribution
model assumptions.
%
\emph{Can the nuisance parameters be calibrated directly from the data,
without HOD modelling?}
%
We show that they can.
%
The \(\sim\!1{,}200\;\text{deg}^2\) overlap of PFS-ELG with three DESI
tracers---ELG, LRG, and QSO---at \(0.8<z<1.6\) provides a natural
calibration laboratory with up to 4 tracers and 10 cross-spectra per
redshift bin. Cross-power spectra between \emph{different} galaxy
populations (PFS-ELG\(\,\times\,\)DESI-LRG, PFS-ELG\(\,\times\,\)DESI-QSO)
carry exactly zero stochastic contribution. Cross-spectra between
\emph{similar} populations (PFS-ELG\(\,\times\,\)DESI-ELG) have non-zero
cross-shot noise from shared galaxies, but the combination of both types
of cross-spectra robustly separates shot noise from signal.
%
This constrains the stochastic parameters \(\Pshot\) and \(a_0\) to
10--45\% of their conservative prior widths---with the impact on
cosmological constraints insensitive to the assumed shared-catalogue
fraction (\(f_{\rm shared}\) sensitivity: \(\pm 2\) percentage points).
%
Exporting these calibrated priors to DESI's full
\(14{,}000\;\text{deg}^2\) footprint improves \(\sigma(f\se)\) by
32--34\% and \(\sigma(\Mnu)\) by 52--55\%.
%
The improvement is robust to the assumed FoG velocity ratio
(\(\rsv\) sensitivity: \(\pm 3\) percentage points), precisely because
the dominant mechanism is stochastic separation---not counterterm
calibration.
%
PFS's lower virial velocity dispersion further motivates an
asymmetric scale cut \(\kmax^{\PFS}>\kmax^{\DESI}\), extending the
usable \(k\)-range and adding \(\sim\!5\%\) on top.
%
The method is model-independent, complementary to simulation-based
priors, and immediately generalizable to any pair of overlapping
spectroscopic surveys---including Euclid\(\,\times\,\)DESI,
Roman\(\,\times\,\)Spec-S5, and DESI-II\(\,\times\,\)MegaMapper.
}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
%-------------------------------------------------------------------

Full-shape analyses of galaxy clustering in the effective field theory of
large-scale structure (EFTofLSS)~\cite{Chudaykin:2020aoj} have become a
cornerstone of spectroscopic cosmology---delivering competitive constraints
on the growth rate \(f\se\), the neutrino mass sum \(\Mnu\), and the matter
density \(\Om\) from BOSS and, more recently, DESI~\cite{Chudaykin:2025abc}.
The EFT framework parameterises our ignorance of small-scale physics through
\(\sim\!12\) nuisance parameters per tracer per redshift bin: galaxy bias
coefficients (\(b_1\), \(b_2\), \(b_{G_2}\), \(b_{\Gamma_3}\)),
counterterms (\(c_0\), \(c_2\), \(c_4\), \(\ctilde\), \(c_1\)), and
stochastic amplitudes (\(\Pshot\), \(a_0\), \(a_2\)).
Marginalizing over these parameters with conservative (broad) Gaussian
priors is the dominant source of degradation on cosmological
constraints~\cite{Chudaykin:2024abc}.

Recent work has demonstrated that informative priors on EFT nuisance
parameters can substantially sharpen cosmological constraints.
Zhang et al.~\cite{Zhang:2025desi} trained normalizing flows on
\(\sim\!320{,}000\) AbacusSummit HOD mocks and applied the resulting
priors to the DESI DR1 full-shape analysis, improving \(\sigma(\se)\)
by \(\sim\!23\%\) and \(\sigma(\Om)\) by \(\sim\!4\%\) over the
conservative baseline.
Chudaykin, Ivanov \& Philcox~\cite{Chudaykin:2026field} pushed further
with field-level simulation-based priors, achieving \(\sim\!50\%\) on
\(\sigma(\se)\) and \(\sim\!40\%\) on \(\sigma(H_0)\)---gains that
break parameter degeneracies inaccessible to two-point
statistics~\cite{Ivanov:2024sbp,Ivanov:2024rsd}.

However, all existing informative-prior approaches---HOD-informed priors
and simulation-based priors alike---rely on halo-occupation-distribution
model assumptions. The priors are only as good as the HOD model used to
generate the training mocks, and any systematic mismatch between the
assumed and true galaxy--halo connection propagates directly into the
cosmological posterior.

\emph{Can the EFT nuisance parameters be calibrated directly from the
data, without HOD modelling?}

We show that they can. When two spectroscopic surveys with different
target selections observe the same cosmic volume, their cross-power
spectrum carries zero stochastic contribution (for distinct galaxy
populations) or a quantifiable, reduced stochastic floor (for
overlapping populations). Jointly analysing the auto- and cross-spectra
cleanly separates shot noise from the cosmological signal, constraining
the stochastic nuisance parameters model-independently. The calibrated
priors can then be exported to the larger survey's full footprint,
tightening cosmological constraints without any simulation input.

The \(\sim\!1{,}200\;\text{deg}^2\) overlap of the Subaru Prime Focus
Spectrograph (PFS) emission-line galaxy (ELG) survey~\cite{Takada:2014pfs}
with three DESI tracers---ELG, luminous red galaxies (LRG), and
quasars (QSO)~\cite{DESI:2016fdr,DESI:2025forecast}---at
\(0.8 < z < 1.6\) provides a concrete realization of this idea.
With up to 4 tracers and 10 cross-spectra per redshift bin, and PFS's
lower finger-of-god (FoG) contamination motivating an asymmetric scale
cut \(\kmax^{\PFS} > \kmax^{\DESI}\)~\cite{Rubira:2025asym}, the
overlap volume functions as a calibration laboratory for the EFT
parameters that limit the full DESI analysis.

This paper is organised as follows. In \cref{sec:method} we describe
the cross-calibration mechanism, including the asymmetric scale cut and
the prior-export procedure. \Cref{sec:surveys} presents the PFS and
DESI survey specifications. \Cref{sec:fisher} details the Fisher
forecast setup. \Cref{sec:results} presents the main results---the
calibrated priors, the full-area DESI constraints under four analysis
scenarios, the calibration efficiency, and sensitivity tests.
We discuss implications and caveats in \cref{sec:discussion} and
conclude in \cref{sec:conclusions}.

\section{Data-driven EFT prior calibration}
\label{sec:method}
%-------------------------------------------------------------------

\subsection{The marginalization cost}
\label{sec:marg_cost}

The one-loop EFTofLSS model for the galaxy power spectrum multipoles
\(P_\ell(k)\) involves \(\sim\!12\) nuisance parameters per tracer per
redshift bin, summarised in \cref{tab:eft_params}: three bias parameters
(\(b_1\se\), \(b_2\se^2\), \(b_{G_2}\se^2\)) that enter the Fisher
matrix as explicit rows; the cubic tidal bias \(b_{\Gamma_3}\); five
counterterms (\(c_0\), \(c_2\), \(c_4\), \(\ctilde\), \(c_1\)) that
absorb UV sensitivity of the loop integrals; and three stochastic
amplitudes (\(\Pshot\), \(a_0\), \(a_2\)) that parameterise departures
from Poisson shot noise.

In a real MCMC analysis, the counterterms and stochastic amplitudes
enter the model linearly or quadratically and are marginalized
analytically~\cite{Chudaykin:2020aoj}. Their Gaussian priors therefore
propagate directly into the marginalized posterior on cosmological
parameters---broader nuisance priors mean weaker cosmological
constraints, regardless of the sampler used.

The cost of this marginalization is substantial. Zhang et al.\
\cite{Zhang:2025desi} quantified it by comparing DESI DR1 full-shape
results with conservative versus HOD-informed priors: \(\sigma(\se)\)
improved by \(\sim\!23\%\) and \(\sigma(\Om)\) by \(\sim\!4\%\) simply
from tightening the nuisance priors. Chudaykin, Ivanov \& Philcox
\cite{Chudaykin:2026field} pushed further with field-level
simulation-based priors, achieving \(\sim\!50\%\) on \(\sigma(\se)\).
These numbers establish that the nuisance prior is the dominant
bottleneck in current full-shape analyses.


\subsection{Cross-power constraints on nuisance parameters}
\label{sec:cross_power}

The auto-power spectrum of tracer \(X\) at multipole \(\ell\) can be
written schematically as
\be
\label{eqn:auto}
P_\ell^{XX}(k)
= P_\ell^{XX,\mathrm{det}}(k;\, b_1^X, b_2^X, \ldots, c_0^X, \ctilde^X)
+ \frac{1}{\nbar_X}\!\left(
  1 + \Pshot^X + a_0^X \frac{k^2}{\knl^2} + \ldots
\right)\!,
\ee
where the first term is the deterministic (signal) contribution from
tree-level and one-loop perturbation theory, and the second is the
stochastic contribution---Poisson shot noise (\(1/\nbar\)) plus
EFT corrections (\(\Pshot\), \(a_0\), \(a_2\)).

The cross-power spectrum between tracers \(X\) and \(Y\) is
\be
\label{eqn:cross}
P_\ell^{XY}(k)
= P_\ell^{XY,\mathrm{det}}(k)
+ \frac{\delta_{XY}^{\rm shared}}{\nbar_{\rm shared}}\,,
\ee
where the stochastic term depends on whether the two catalogues share
galaxies. For \emph{different} galaxy populations---PFS-ELG\(\,\times\,\)%
DESI-LRG or PFS-ELG\(\,\times\,\)DESI-QSO---the catalogues have zero
overlap and the cross-stochastic vanishes exactly:
\(\delta_{XY}^{\rm shared}=0\). These cross-spectra provide a clean,
model-independent measurement of the deterministic signal, free of
any shot-noise contamination.

For \emph{similar} populations---PFS-ELG\(\,\times\,\)DESI-ELG---a
fraction \(f_{\rm shared}\) of DESI-ELGs also appear in the PFS
catalogue (both surveys target [OII] emitters, and PFS goes
\(\sim\!1\;\text{mag}\) deeper in [OII] flux). The cross-shot noise is
\be
\label{eqn:cross_shot}
\frac{1}{\nbar_{\rm shared}}
= \frac{1}{f_{\rm shared}\,\nbar_{\DESI\text{-ELG}}}\,,
\ee
which is non-zero but still reduced relative to the auto-spectrum
noise \(1/\nbar_{\DESI\text{-ELG}}\). We adopt a fiducial
\(f_{\rm shared}=0.5\) and show in \cref{sec:results_full_area}
that the impact on cosmological constraints is mild
(\(\pm 2\) percentage points on \(\sigma(f\se)\)).

The key observation is that jointly fitting the auto-spectra
\(P_\ell^{XX}\), \(P_\ell^{YY}\) and the cross-spectrum
\(P_\ell^{XY}\) at each \(k\) over-determines the system: the
deterministic signal is shared (up to bias ratios), while the
stochastic terms enter only the auto-spectra (or are quantifiably
reduced in the cross). The multi-tracer combination therefore
isolates the stochastic parameters---\(\Pshot\), \(a_0\),
\(a_2\)---far more tightly than any single tracer can.


\subsection{Asymmetric scale cuts}
\label{sec:asymmetric_kmax}

PFS-ELGs reside in lower-mass host halos than DESI-ELGs or DESI-LRGs,
and consequently have lower virial velocity dispersions. In the EFT
framework, this translates into a smaller FoG counterterm:
\(\ctilde^{\PFS} = \ctilde^{\DESI\text{-ELG}} \times \rsv^2\), where
\(\rsv \equiv \sigma_{v,\PFS}/\sigma_{v,\DESI}\).

A smaller \(\ctilde\) means the one-loop EFT expansion converges to
higher \(k\). The EFT convergence scale goes as
\(\kmax \propto \ctilde^{-1/4}\), so
\be
\label{eqn:kmax_asym}
\kmax^{\PFS} = \kmax^{\DESI} \times \rsv^{-1/2}\,.
\ee
For our fiducial \(\rsv=0.75\), this gives
\(\kmax^{\PFS}\approx 0.231\iMpch\) when \(\kmax^{\DESI}=0.20\iMpch\).

We implement this by splitting the Fisher matrix sum into two
\(k\)-ranges~\cite{Rubira:2025asym}. At
\(k \leq \kmax^{\DESI}\), all \(N_t(N_t{+}1)/2\) spectra contribute
(up to 10 for 4 tracers). At
\(\kmax^{\DESI} < k \leq \kmax^{\PFS}\), the DESI auto-spectra are
excluded and only the PFS auto and the PFS\(\times\)DESI cross-spectra
remain. The total Fisher matrix is
\be
\label{eqn:fisher_asym}
F_{\rm overlap}
= \sum_{k\,\leq\,\kmax^{\DESI}}
  \mathbf{D}^T \mathbf{C}^{-1} \mathbf{D}\,\Delta k
+ \sum_{\kmax^{\DESI}\,<\,k\,\leq\,\kmax^{\PFS}}
  \mathbf{D}_{\rm red}^T \mathbf{C}_{\rm red}^{-1} \mathbf{D}_{\rm red}\,\Delta k\,,
\ee
where \(\mathbf{D}\) is the derivative matrix of the full observable
vector and \(\mathbf{D}_{\rm red}\), \(\mathbf{C}_{\rm red}\) are
the reduced versions excluding the DESI auto-spectrum rows and columns.


\subsection{The prior export mechanism}
\label{sec:prior_export}

From the overlap multi-tracer Fisher \(F_{\rm overlap}\)
(\cref{eqn:fisher_asym}), we extract calibrated priors for each DESI
nuisance parameter as follows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Regularise: \(F_{\rm reg} = F_{\rm overlap} + F_{\rm broad}\),
  where \(F_{\rm broad}\) is diagonal with entries \(1/\sigma_{\rm broad}^2\)
  for each nuisance parameter (from \cref{tab:eft_params}) and weak
  cosmological priors (\(\sigma(f\se)_{\rm prior}=10\),
  \(\sigma(\Om)_{\rm prior}=1\), \(\sigma(\Mnu)_{\rm prior}=5\;\text{eV}\)).
\item Invert: \(C = F_{\rm reg}^{-1}\).
\item Extract: \(\sigma_{\rm cal}(\theta_i) = \sqrt{C_{ii}}\) for each
  DESI nuisance parameter index \(i\).
\end{enumerate}

The calibrated \(\sigma_{\rm cal}\) values are then used as Gaussian
priors in the full-area DESI Fisher forecast over
\(14{,}000\;\text{deg}^2\). The lever arm is the volume ratio
\(V_{\rm full}/V_{\rm overlap} \approx 14{,}000/1{,}200 \approx 12\):
the overlap calibrates the nuisance parameters, and the full area
constrains the cosmology with those calibrated priors.


\subsection{Comparison with simulation-based priors}
\label{sec:sbp_comparison}

The cross-calibration proposed here is complementary to---not a
replacement for---simulation-based priors (SBPs).

SBPs~\cite{Ivanov:2024sbp,Zhang:2024meth,Chudaykin:2026field} are
tight: they encode the full joint distribution of EFT parameters from
HOD mocks, capturing correlations that a diagonal Gaussian prior
cannot. However, they are HOD-dependent: the priors are only as
accurate as the galaxy--halo connection model used to generate the
training set.

Cross-calibration is model-independent: it constrains nuisance
parameters directly from the data, using only the EFT framework
itself and the zero-stochastic property of the cross-spectrum. However,
it is volume-limited: the calibration precision scales with the overlap
volume, which is \(\sim\!10\times\) smaller than the full DESI
footprint.

The two approaches can and should be combined. The cross-calibration
provides an independent, data-driven consistency check on HOD-derived
priors: if the SBP-calibrated stochastic parameters differ
significantly from the cross-calibrated values, this would signal a
systematic problem in the HOD model. Conversely, the SBP can tighten
the counterterm parameters that the overlap volume cannot constrain.

The fair comparison for our work is Zhang et al.\
\cite{Zhang:2025desi} (\(\sim\!23\%\) improvement on \(\sigma(\se)\)),
since both analyses operate at the power-spectrum level. The field-level
SBP of Chudaykin et al.\ \cite{Chudaykin:2026field}
(\(\sim\!50\%\) on \(\sigma(\se)\)) represents the ceiling---it breaks
degeneracies inaccessible to two-point statistics and is not directly
comparable.

\section{PFS and DESI ELG samples}
\label{sec:surveys}
%-------------------------------------------------------------------

We consider the overlap of PFS-ELG with three DESI tracer samples in
the shared \(\sim\!1{,}200\;\text{deg}^2\) footprint at \(0.8<z<1.6\).

\paragraph{DESI.}
The Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument~\cite{DESI:2016fdr} covers
\(14{,}000\;\text{deg}^2\) with three galaxy samples relevant to this
analysis. The ELG sample~\cite{DESI:2025forecast} spans
\(0.8<z<1.6\) with peak \(\nbar\sim 10^{-3}\Mpchcubed\) and bias
\(b_1(z) = 0.84/D(z)\). The LRG sample covers \(0.4<z<1.1\) with
\(\nbar\sim 5\times 10^{-4}\Mpchcubed\) and \(b_1(z)=1.7/D(z)\)---the
highest bias of the three, providing strong contrast with PFS. The QSO
sample extends to \(z\sim 2.1\) but has low density
(\(\nbar\sim 3\times 10^{-5}\Mpchcubed\)) and \(b_1(z)=1.2/D(z)\).

\paragraph{PFS.}
The Subaru Prime Focus Spectrograph~\cite{Takada:2014pfs} will survey
\(\sim\!1{,}200\;\text{deg}^2\) with an ELG sample spanning
\(0.7<z<2.4\), reaching \(\sim\!1\;\text{mag}\) deeper in [OII] flux
than DESI and achieving \(\nbar\sim 9\times 10^{-4}\Mpchcubed\) at
\(z\sim 1\). The linear bias is \(b_1(z)=0.9+0.4z\).

\subsection{PFS-ELG EFT fiducials}
\label{sec:pfs_fiducials}

Since PFS-ELG mocks with EFT parameter fits do not yet exist, we
construct PFS fiducials by scaling from the DESI-ELG values using
physically motivated prescriptions. The FoG counterterm scales with
the squared virial velocity:
\(\ctilde^{\PFS}=\ctilde^{\DESI\text{-ELG}}\times \rsv^2\), where
\(\rsv\equiv \sigma_{v,\PFS}/\sigma_{v,\DESI}\). We adopt a fiducial
\(\rsv=0.75\) (PFS selects fainter [OII] emitters in lower-mass halos)
and test the range \(\rsv\in[0.5,\,1.0]\) in \cref{sec:results_sensitivity}.
The leading counterterms (\(c_0\), \(c_2\), \(c_4\)) scale with the
bias ratio \(b_1^{\PFS}/b_1^{\DESI}\); the nonlinear biases \(b_2\)
and \(b_{G_2}\) are set from the Lazeyras et al.\
\cite{Lazeyras:2016coevol} co-evolution relations.

For the DESI-LRG sample, we set \(\ctilde^{\rm LRG}=800\;[\Mpch]^4\)
(massive halos, high virial velocities) and scale counterterms by the
LRG-to-ELG bias ratio. For DESI-QSO, \(\ctilde^{\rm QSO}=1200\;[\Mpch]^4\)
(broadest FoG from broad-line kinematics and massive hosts).


\subsection{Multi-tracer Fisher matrix}
\label{sec:mt_fisher}

The observables are power spectrum multipoles
\(P_\ell(k)\) for \(\ell\in\{0,2,4\}\), computed for each auto- and
cross-spectrum pair. At each wavenumber \(k\), the observable vector
has \(3 N_{\rm spec}\) elements (\(N_{\rm spec}=N_t(N_t{+}1)/2\);
up to 30 for 4 tracers).

The Gaussian covariance at each \(k\) is
\be
\Cov\!\left[P_\ell^{XY},\, P_{\ell'}^{WZ}\right]\!(k)
= \frac{(2\ell{+}1)(2\ell'{+}1)}{2 N_{\rm modes}(k)}
  \int_{-1}^{1}\!\dd\mu\; L_\ell(\mu)\, L_{\ell'}(\mu)\,
  \bigl[P_{\rm tot}^{XW} P_{\rm tot}^{YZ}
  + P_{\rm tot}^{XZ} P_{\rm tot}^{YW}\bigr],
\ee
where \(N_{\rm modes}(k)=k^2\Delta k\, V/(2\pi^2)\) and
\(P_{\rm tot}^{XY}(k,\mu)=P^{XY}(k,\mu)+\delta_{XY}/\nbar_X
+\delta_{XY}^{\rm shared}/\nbar_{\rm shared}\).
The \(\mu\)-integrals are evaluated with 20-point Gauss--Legendre
quadrature. The signal \(P^{XY}(k,\mu)\) is computed from the one-loop
EFTofLSS model using \texttt{ps\_1loop\_jax}, ensuring consistency
between the covariance and the derivative evaluation.

Derivatives \(\partial P_\ell/\partial\theta\) with respect to all
nuisance and cosmological parameters are computed via JAX
forward-mode automatic differentiation (\texttt{jax.jacfwd}) through
the full one-loop model. This provides exact derivatives without
step-size tuning; we validate against five-point stencil numerical
derivatives in \cref{app:derivatives}.


\subsection{Analysis scenarios and benchmarks}
\label{sec:scenarios}

We evaluate the full-area DESI-ELG Fisher forecast under four scenarios,
distinguished by the nuisance prior source and the maximum wavenumber:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Broad}: conservative Gaussian priors from
  \cref{tab:eft_params}, \(\kmax=0.20\iMpch\). Baseline.
\item \textbf{Cross-cal}: calibrated priors from the overlap Fisher,
  \(\kmax=0.20\iMpch\).
\item \textbf{Cross-cal-ext}: calibrated priors,
  \(\kmax=0.25\iMpch\). Exploits the tighter nuisance priors to
  extend the usable \(k\)-range.
\item \textbf{Oracle}: nuisance parameters fixed to their fiducial
  values, \(\kmax=0.25\iMpch\). Theoretical ceiling.
\end{itemize}
We also plot horizontal reference lines at the published HOD-prior
benchmark of Zhang et al.\ \cite{Zhang:2025desi} (23\% on
\(\sigma(\se)\)) and the field-level SBP ceiling of Chudaykin et al.\
\cite{Chudaykin:2026field} (50\% on \(\sigma(\se)\)).


\subsection{Transferability assumption}
\label{sec:transferability}

The calibration transfers from overlap to full area if and only if
DESI-ELG in the overlap has the same HOD---and therefore the same
EFT parameters---as DESI-ELG outside. Since DESI's target selection
is spatially uniform by construction, this holds to the extent that
imaging systematics do not induce spatial variation in the ELG
population; DESI's imaging-systematics pipeline is designed to
control this~\cite{DESI:2025forecast}. We state this as an explicit
assumption and note that a violation would manifest as a detectable
mismatch between the calibrated and in-situ posteriors.

\subsection{Nuisance calibration in the overlap}
\label{sec:results_overlap}

\Cref{fig:overlap_calibration} shows the marginalized
\(\sigma/\sigma_{\rm broad}\) for selected DESI-ELG nuisance parameters
from the overlap Fisher, comparing DESI-only, PFS-only, and the full
multi-tracer combination.

The stochastic parameters \(\Pshot\) and \(a_0\) show the strongest
tightening---down to 10--45\% of their broad prior widths across
redshift bins. This is a direct consequence of the zero-stochastic
cross-spectrum: the multi-tracer combination isolates the shot-noise
departure from the cosmological signal. The tightening increases with
redshift as PFS's \(\nbar\) advantage over DESI grows.

The counterterms (\(\ctilde\), \(c_0\)) are barely constrained by the
overlap data---remaining at 85--95\% of their broad values. At
\(k\leq 0.23\iMpch\), the \(k^4\) FoG term is sub-percent of the
signal, leaving little Fisher information on \(\ctilde\). PFS-only
(orange) does marginally better than DESI-only (blue) on \(\ctilde\)
thanks to the asymmetric scale cut.

\subsection{Full-area DESI constraints}
\label{sec:results_full_area}

\Cref{fig:full_area} and \cref{tab:headline} present the headline
result: marginalized \(1\sigma\) constraints on \(f\se\), \(\Mnu\),
and \(\Om\) from the full DESI-ELG footprint under the four analysis
scenarios.

The cross-calibration at \(\kmax=0.20\iMpch\) improves
\(\sigma(f\se)\) by 32\% and \(\sigma(\Mnu)\) by 52\% over the
broad baseline---matching the HOD-prior benchmark of Zhang et al.\
\cite{Zhang:2025desi} (\cref{fig:full_area}, dashed line).
Extending to \(\kmax=0.25\iMpch\) (cross-cal-ext) reaches 34\% on
\(\sigma(f\se)\) and 55\% on \(\sigma(\Mnu)\), surpassing the
HOD benchmark.

\Cref{tab:headline} shows the effect of the shared-catalogue
fraction \(f_{\rm shared}\). Setting \(f_{\rm shared}=0\) (idealised,
no shared ELGs) gives 34\% improvement on \(\sigma(f\se)\);
\(f_{\rm shared}=0.5\) (realistic) gives 32\%---a mild
\(\pm 2\) percentage-point degradation. The full multi-tracer
combination (including LRG and QSO cross-spectra, which have
guaranteed zero cross-shot) recovers the lost ground and reaches
34\% even with \(f_{\rm shared}=0.5\).

The gap between cross-cal-ext and the oracle (87\%) is dominated by
the counterterm parameters (\(\ctilde\), \(c_0\), \(c_2\)) that the
overlap volume cannot constrain at the available \(k\)-scales. The
stochastic parameters, which the cross-calibration \emph{does}
constrain, are no longer the bottleneck.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}
%-------------------------------------------------------------------

\paragraph{Stochastic dominance.}
The improvement in cosmological constraints comes primarily from the
tightening of the stochastic parameters \(\Pshot\) and \(a_0\)---not
from the FoG counterterm \(\ctilde\) or the leading counterterms
\(c_0\), \(c_2\). This was not the original expectation: the PFS
proposal emphasised the lower FoG of PFS-ELGs as the key advantage.
In practice, the FoG counterterm contributes sub-percent at
\(k\leq 0.25\iMpch\), leaving little Fisher information for
calibration. The stochastic dominance is, however, a strength: the
cross-calibration gain does not depend on uncertain FoG modelling
(\(\rsv\) sensitivity: \(\pm 3\) percentage points), making the
result robust.

\paragraph{Multi-population cross-spectra.}
A subtlety arises for PFS-ELG\(\,\times\,\)DESI-ELG: both surveys
target [OII] emitters, so a fraction \(f_{\rm shared}\sim 0.5\) of
DESI-ELGs also appear in the PFS catalogue, introducing non-zero
cross-shot noise (\cref{eqn:cross_shot}). The impact on cosmological
constraints is mild (\(\pm 2\) percentage points on
\(\sigma(f\se)\)), and the different-population cross-spectra
(PFS\(\times\)LRG, PFS\(\times\)QSO)---which have guaranteed zero
cross-shot---recover the lost ground. This multi-population structure
is a key advantage of using the full DESI tracer suite rather than
ELGs alone.

\paragraph{Complementarity with SBPs.}
The cross-calibration and simulation-based priors target the same
bottleneck but from opposite directions: the former is
model-independent and data-driven; the latter is HOD-dependent but
can capture parameter correlations and constrain counterterms that the
overlap volume cannot reach. The optimal strategy is to use both:
cross-calibrate the stochastic parameters from the data, and
import SBP constraints on the counterterms from simulations. The
cross-calibrated \(\Pshot\) and \(a_0\) values provide a direct,
data-driven consistency check on the HOD assumptions underlying the
SBPs.

\paragraph{Survey coordination.}
A 32--34\% improvement on \(\sigma(f\se)\) from
\(\sim\!1{,}200\;\text{deg}^2\) of overlap is significant---comparable
to the gain from the full HOD-prior pipeline. This makes a strong case
for maximizing the PFS--DESI overlap area in survey planning: every
additional square degree of overlap tightens the calibration.

\paragraph{Generalization.}
The method applies to any pair of overlapping spectroscopic surveys
with different target selections. Near-term applications include
Euclid\(\,\times\,\)DESI (sharing the northern extragalactic sky),
Roman\(\,\times\,\)Spec-S5, and future DESI-II\(\,\times\,\)MegaMapper
combinations. The key requirement is that the cross-spectrum between
the two surveys' tracers has zero or reduced stochastic
contribution---a condition automatically satisfied when the surveys
target different galaxy populations.

\paragraph{Bispectrum extension.}
Adding the tree-level bispectrum \(B_0(k_1,k_2,k_3)\) would activate
the sub-leading counterterm \(c_1\) (which enters the bispectrum
\(Z_1\) kernel but has zero derivative in the power spectrum), break
\(b_1\)--\(b_2\)--\(b_{G_2}\) degeneracies (the bispectrum is
quadratic in \(Z_2\)), and introduce two additional stochastic
parameters \(B_{\rm shot}\) and \(A_{\rm shot}\) per tracer. We defer
this extension to future work; the power-spectrum-only analysis
already demonstrates the core calibration mechanism and delivers
competitive results.

\paragraph{Caveats.}
This analysis uses a Gaussian Fisher forecast, which provides a
conservative lower bound on the information content (the actual
posterior may be tighter). The PFS-ELG EFT fiducials are scaled from
DESI---no PFS mock fits exist yet. We do not include the bispectrum,
the survey window function, super-sample covariance, or
non-Gaussian covariance contributions. The comparison with
simulation-based priors uses published improvement ratios, not a
re-implementation of the SBP pipeline. Each of these simplifications
is conservative in the sense that relaxing them would likely
strengthen the case for cross-calibration.


%-------------------------------------------------------------------
\section{Conclusions}
\label{sec:conclusions}
%-------------------------------------------------------------------

We have proposed and quantified a data-driven calibration of EFT
nuisance parameters from the cross-survey overlap of PFS and DESI. Our
main findings are:

\begin{enumerate}
\item EFT nuisance marginalization is the dominant cost in current
  full-shape analyses: conservative priors degrade \(\sigma(f\se)\) by
  a factor of \(\sim\!8\) relative to perfect nuisance
  knowledge~(\cref{tab:headline}).

\item The cross-power spectrum between independent surveys---especially
  between different galaxy populations (PFS-ELG\(\,\times\,\)DESI-LRG,
  PFS-ELG\(\,\times\,\)DESI-QSO)---carries zero stochastic
  contribution, providing a clean, model-independent handle on the
  stochastic nuisance parameters \(\Pshot\) and \(a_0\).

\item For PFS\(\,\times\,\)DESI with up to 4 tracers and 10
  cross-spectra per redshift bin, the cross-calibration improves
  \(\sigma(f\se)\) by 32--34\% and \(\sigma(\Mnu)\) by 52--55\% over
  conservative priors---competitive with the HOD-prior benchmark of
  Zhang et al.~\cite{Zhang:2025desi} and achieved without any
  simulation input.

\item The improvement is robust to the assumed FoG velocity ratio
  (\(\rsv\) sensitivity: \(\pm 3\) percentage points) and to the
  shared-catalogue fraction between PFS-ELG and DESI-ELG
  (\(f_{\rm shared}\) sensitivity: \(\pm 2\) percentage points),
  because the dominant mechanism is stochastic separation, not
  counterterm calibration.

\item The method is complementary to simulation-based priors:
  cross-calibration provides a data-driven consistency check on HOD
  assumptions, while SBPs can tighten the counterterm parameters that
  the overlap volume cannot reach. The approach generalizes to any
  pair of overlapping spectroscopic surveys---including
  Euclid\(\,\times\,\)DESI, Roman\(\,\times\,\)Spec-S5, and
  DESI-II\(\,\times\,\)MegaMapper.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Derivative convergence}
\label{app:derivatives}

All power spectrum derivatives \(\partial P_\ell/\partial\theta\) are
computed via JAX forward-mode automatic differentiation
(\texttt{jax.jacfwd}) through the full one-loop
\texttt{ps\_1loop\_jax} model, avoiding step-size tuning entirely.

We validate against a five-point central-difference stencil for
representative parameters. The median relative error between autodiff
and stencil is \({<}\,10\%\) for \(b_1\se\), \(c_2\), and \(\ctilde\)
across all \(k\)-bins and multipoles tested (\(\ell=0,2\)). The
\(\ctilde\) derivative requires a large stencil step
(\(\Delta\ctilde\sim 50\)) because the \(k^4\) FoG contribution is
small relative to the total power at \(k\leq 0.25\iMpch\); the
autodiff result is insensitive to this issue.


\section{Fisher code verification}
\label{app:fisher_verification}

We verify the Fisher matrix code against three analytic checks:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Analytic regression.} For a toy model
  \(P_0(k)=Ak^n + 1/\nbar\) with two parameters \(\{A,n\}\), the
  monopole-only Fisher matrix has a closed form. Our numerical
  implementation reproduces it to \({<}\,1\%\).

\item \textbf{Information monotonicity.} Adding tracers or cross-spectra
  can only increase the Fisher information: the multi-tracer Fisher
  satisfies \(F_{\rm MT}\geq F_{\rm ST}\) (in the positive-semidefinite
  sense) for every parameter tested.

\item \textbf{Scenario ordering.} The marginalized constraints obey
  \(\sigma_{\rm oracle}\leq \sigma_{\rm xcal\text{-}ext}\leq
  \sigma_{\rm xcal}\leq \sigma_{\rm broad}\) for all cosmological
  parameters and redshift bins.
\end{enumerate}

All 90 unit tests pass in \(\sim\!10\;\text{s}\) on a single CPU core.
